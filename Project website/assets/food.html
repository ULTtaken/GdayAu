Iconic Australian Foods

Tim Tam
It is heard that the best way to eat Tim Tams is: bite the diagonal corners and then take a cup of coffee, tea or hot chocolate.
Another interesting way is: use it as a straw and drink through biscuits. Before it gets too soggy and falls into your drink, you will enjoy the soft and warm chocolate.
Pavlova
Australians and New Zealanders have been competing for the title of Pavlova's "inventor" - a meringue cake base with whipped cream and fruit.
In Australia, people can easily find it at a cake shop or a bakery. People can also buy it from some large supermarket chains (bread or frozen dessert section).
Grilled Kangaroo
Kangaroo is one of the healthiest meats. From the taste point of view, it is closest to beef. However, kangaroos contain more protein and iron than beef, with less calories and fat content. In addition, it is also a good source of omega-3.
Barramundi
Australian barramundi, a name which means ‘large-scaled river fish’ in the Aboriginal language in the Rockhampton area. Whatever fried or grilled, its seared skin-sides are heathier alternatives than battered fish and a true Aussie meal.
Going to Queensland will bring you some of the best fish in the country.
Lamingtons
Lamingtons are widely recognized as the ‘National Cake of Australia’. Lamington is a moderate square sponge dipped in chocolate and coated with a dry coconut.
They can be perfectly matched with a delicious bush tea or one of Melbourne's world-famous coffees, and can be found at all the great bakeries and cafes.
Chicken Parmigian
This classic Australian chicken dish – derived from Italian American cuisine - is the main product of almost all bars in the country.
At the beginning, it is based on eggplant, which are still suitable for vegetarians. And now, it has been developed into chicken schnitzel with tomato sauce and melted cheese.
