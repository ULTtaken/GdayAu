Do these things in Australia

Australia is an interesting and beautiful country.
Do the right things to enjoy this fantastic world.

DO

+

Do learn Australian Slangs

Australians use a lot of slangs which you would have never heard before.

you can learn Aussie slangs from our slang translator and even attempt a slang quiz to test you slang knowledge!!!

Just go to Slang and explore a whole new world of Aussie Slangs.

Aussie shorten words

+

Slip, Slop, Slap, Sleek

Australia has one of the highest UV index in the world.

During a regular summer noon, the UV index values are in excess of 12-14 and can even reach 16-17 at some places.

The Cancer Council Australia and SunSmart has an ongoing campaign to keep people protected.

Slip, Slop, Slap, Seek, Slide

+

Carry your ID Card

If you are looking to enjoy the night and get into a club, don’t forget to carry you ID card with you.

18 years is the minimum drinking age in Australia.

If asked, you must show either your passport or an Australian Driving licence.

Carry ID

+

Do Try Vegemite

Vegemite is the most loved spread in Australia.

It is made from yeast.

Try experimenting vegemite and butter for your next breakfast. Here’s a tip, more butter and a light smear of vegemite is unbeatable.

Try Vegemite

+

Do Drink Coffee

Australians are coffee snobs. They know their baristas well and go out to independent coffee shops.

Maybe the next time you go out to a coffee shop, you find a new friend!!

Drink coffee

+

Be Aware That Australia Is A Big Country

Australia is the sixth largest country in the world with a total area of 7,692,024 square km.

Australia has three different time zones and some states also have day light savings.

Australia has around 10000 beaches!!!

Get to know Australia

+

Do have Brunch

Australians love having breakfast out in the sun along with a coffee.

You can see them enjoying smashed Avacados with fetta, basil on sourdough, acai bowls, ricotta hotcakes with honeycomb butter and good old bacon and eggs along with coffee.

Go for a brunch

+

Swim only between flags

Majority of the people who die drowning are either international students or tourists who are unaware of sea conditions that can quickly change.

Everyone should swim only between the flags at patrolled beaches. Although it is not a rule, but it is something that you should do for your safety and for loved ones.

Swim between the flags

+

Understand Australian Road Rules

You need to have a valid Driver’s license.

Everyone in the car should wear a seat belt.

Blood Alcohol concentration should not be more than 0.05%.

Understand traffic rules

DON'T

+

It’s ok if you not tip

Tip culture is not as prevalent in Australia as it is in US.

Most Australian’s offer their staff a fair amount of minimum wage, which means they don’t have to rely a lot on tips.

It is more of a personal choice to tip for an excellent service or meal.

Tips

+

Don’t climb Uluru

Although it is not illegal to climb the Uluru currently, it is considered as a disrespectful activity.

Uluru is considered sacred by the Aborigines and ask the tourists to respect their culture and wishes by not climbing Uluru.

Effective 26 October 2019, marking the 34th anniversary of the Uluru hand- back, the climb will be closed.

Climb Uluru

+

Don’t Drink and Drive

Australia has strict laws for drunk driving and regular alcohol and drug tests are carried out by the police randomly.

The blood alcohol limit is 0.05% and for learners and provisional licenses, the limit is 0.00%.

In simple words, you can have 2 standard drinks and then wait for at least an hour before you can get your car out on road to stay below 0.05%.

Drunk-driving

+

Don’t travel without insurance

Medical expenses and booking cancellations can be really expensive in Australia.

Make sure you are covered from these unexpected expenses to save your wallet.

Travel without insurance

+

Don’t pet a Koala

Australia has some of the most unique wildlife in the world.

In interest of animal welfare, it is not allowed to pat Australian wildlife such as Koala.

An NSW government regulation even forbids holding of Koala and it is deemed as an illegal activity.

Pet a Koala

+

Don’t smoke in Public

In a recent step taken by a council, smoking has been banned in parts of Melbourne CBD.

Smoking is banned in enclosed work spaces, restaurants, cinema halls etc.

If caught smoking, you may be fined $100.

Smoke in public

+

Don’t jump the Queue

It is rude to jump the Queue.

Make sure you are always in a queue whether you are waiting for a public transport, or it’s a shopping mall or pub.

While the most Australian don’t actually stand in a straight-line sort of queue, you need to take in who was there first and wait your turn.

Jump the queue

+

Don’t throw a cigarette butt irresponsibly

Most of Australian bushfires are caught due to cigarette butts.

Make sure you throw your cigarette butt responsibly at designated places.

Throw a cigarette butt

+

Don’t Litter

"Don’t be a tosser". It’s an Australian slogan which means don’t toss your rubbish on the ground.

Littering is illegal in Australia and you can be fined for it.

Litter
